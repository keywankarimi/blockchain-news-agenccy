### Run: 2026-03-26

#### 1. STABLECOIN PAYMENTS — MAJOR INSTITUTIONAL WAVE

**What happened:**
- SoFi + Mastercard launched SoFiUSD settlement on Mastercard's global payments network (March 3, 2026) — first stablecoin from a U.S. nationally chartered insured bank on a permissionless public blockchain
- PayPal expanded stablecoin payments to 70 countries (March 23, 2026)
- Visa accepts USDC in 80 countries; Mastercard supports USDC merchant settlement
- Stripe made stablecoins the default B2B payment option
- Stablecoin market cap: $311–312B (up ~50% YoY); $30B/day transacted
- Deel launched stablecoin payroll in UK/EU (March 2026) via MoonPay Iron infrastructure
- GENIUS Act passed — federal framework for payment stablecoins; not securities/commodities/deposits

**Why it matters:** Stablecoins are no longer experimental — they are becoming regulated payment instruments endorsed by the largest payment networks in the world. The GENIUS Act creates the legal certainty needed for enterprise adoption at scale.

**Sector:** Stablecoin payments, B2B fintech, payroll
**Buyer/User:** Banks, fintechs, payroll providers, B2B SaaS companies, global businesses with cross-border payment needs
**Startup attractiveness:** INCREASES — regulatory moat now exists for compliant stablecoin issuers/infrastructure; opportunity for middleware between legacy payments and stablecoin rails

- Tokenized RWA value surpassed $26.48B (up ~4x YoY from ~$6.6B)
- 5.25% growth in just the past 30 days

This is the inflection point where regulatory clarity and institutional demand converge — exactly when infrastructure startups have maximum leverage.

**Sector:** Asset tokenization, securities infrastructure, DeFi collateral, capital markets
**Buyer/User:** Asset managers, banks, broker-dealers, fund administrators, compliance teams
**Startup attractiveness:** INCREASES — the compliance and middleware layer (legal wrappers, oracle feeds, on-chain fund admin) is the next buildable surface

#### 3. ZK PROOFS — COMPLIANCE + SCALING CONVERGENCE

**What happened:**
- ZK-based KYC/AML tools moving to production: enterprises can now verify compliance without exposing full customer data
- zkVM ecosystem maturing: RISC Zero, Succinct Labs support Rust/RISC-V, lowering dev barrier
- Succinct's Open Prover Layer (OPL) creates decentralized proving marketplace
- zkEVM deployments accelerating: Linea (LINEA token), Immutable zkEVM live with games, Ronin app-specific zkEVM via Polygon CDK
- Governments exploring ZK-based national identity frameworks
- Cross-chain ZK bridges emerging (no centralized bridge trust required)

**Why it matters:** ZK is solving the enterprise compliance paradox — regulatory transparency + user privacy — simultaneously. As KYC/AML requirements proliferate across stablecoin and RWA markets, ZK compliance tooling becomes a must-have, not a nice-to-have.

**Sector:** Privacy tech, compliance infrastructure, L2 scaling, identity
**Buyer/User:** Regulated DeFi protocols, banks, enterprises building on-chain, government identity programs
**Startup attractiveness:** INCREASES — esp. for ZK compliance middleware (prove you're KYC'd without revealing identity); proving-as-a-service for smaller teams

#### 4. CROSS-CHAIN / INTENT ARCHITECTURE — UX LAYER UNSOLVED

**What happened:**
- Intent-based architectures (NEAR Intents, etc.) going mainstream — users specify outcome, not execution path
- Aggregation becoming "default UX" for bridging — single interface routes across multiple protocols
- Wormhole, Stargate (LayerZero), deBridge leading — deBridge $9B+ transfers, zero exploits
- $2.8B lost in bridge hacks in 2025 — security remains the #1 attack surface
- Chain abstraction layer emerging as the end-state: invisible cross-chain UX
- ZK light-client bridges maturing as the trust-minimized alternative to multisig bridges

**Why it matters:** The multi-chain world is permanent, and fragmentation remains acute. Intent-based routing and chain abstraction are the UX primitives that will unlock the next 100M users — and the infrastructure plumbing for institutional cross-chain RWA movement.

**Sector:** Cross-chain infrastructure, bridging, UX/wallet layer, institutional settlement
**Buyer/User:** Wallets, DeFi protocols, exchanges, institutional desks trading across chains
**Startup attractiveness:** INCREASES for security-focused bridge tooling and intent solvers; decreases for commodity lock-and-mint bridges (already commoditized)
