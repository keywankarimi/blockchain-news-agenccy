## RUN: 2026-03-25

### 1. STABLECOINS & PAYMENTS

**What happened:**
- SoFi and Mastercard announced SoFiUSD as a live settlement option on Mastercard's global network (Mar 3, 2026). SoFiUSD is the first stablecoin from a U.S. nationally chartered bank on a public permissionless blockchain.
- PayPal enabled stablecoin payments across 70 countries (Mar 23, 2026).
- Stablecoin market cap reached ~$312B (up ~50% YoY), with $11T in adjusted transfer volume in 2025.
- Visa and Mastercard now support USDC settlement natively.
- Deel + MoonPay Iron launched stablecoin payroll in UK/EU (Mar 2026).
- GENIUS Act enacted: federal framework for payment stablecoins, classified outside securities/commodities law.

**Why it matters:**
- Stablecoins have crossed the chasm from crypto-native trading tools to mainstream settlement infrastructure. Banks, payroll providers, and card networks are now building on top of them.
- Regulatory clarity (GENIUS Act) unlocks a flood of institutional implementations.

**Sector:** Stablecoin payments, B2B settlement, cross-border remittance, payroll

**Who buys/uses:** Banks, fintechs, enterprises doing cross-border payments, payroll platforms, card networks

**Startup attractiveness:** INCREASES sharply. API-layer plays on top of stablecoin rails (compliance, FX, treasury management) have immediate revenue potential.

### 2. RWA TOKENIZATION

**What happened:**
- Tokenized RWA market grew 4x YoY to $26.4B on-chain. Six asset categories crossed $1B: private credit, commodities, US Treasuries, corporate bonds, non-US gov debt, institutional alt funds.
- Private credit tokenization grew 180% YoY; Centrifuge, Maple, Goldfinch originated $3.2B on-chain.
- MakerDAO (Sky) holds $2B in RWA collateral.
- JPMorgan Onyx processed $900B+ in tokenized repo transactions.
- Novarra BBX expanded institutional tokenization infrastructure (Mar 24, 2026).
- House Financial Services Committee held landmark hearing "Tokenization and the Future of Securities" on Mar 25, 2026.
- SEC + CFTC published joint 68-page interpretive release with five-category token taxonomy (Mar 17, 2026).
- CLARITY Act passed House 294-134; Senate deadline pressure by May.

**Why it matters:**
- RWA tokenization is no longer pilot-stage; it's entering capital markets infrastructure. Congressional + regulatory momentum is accelerating. BlackRock, JPMorgan, Citi, Goldman all committed.
- McKinsey projects $2T by 2030. BIS projects 10% of global GDP tokenized by 2034.

**Sector:** Capital markets, private credit, real estate, treasury management, trade finance

**Who buys/uses:** Asset managers, banks, institutional investors, fund administrators

**Startup attractiveness:** INCREASES. Middleware (compliance rails, KYC/AML hooks, secondary market liquidity tooling, custody connectors) has massive whitespace now that primary issuance infrastructure is maturing.

### 3. DEFI AUTOMATION / AI AGENTS (DeFAI)

**What happened:**
- Orbs launched "Orbs Agentic" on Mar 19, 2026 — a dedicated execution layer for autonomous DeFi agents with cosigned oracle verification and on-chain trust scoring.
- Uniswap Labs released 7 open-source AI agent "skills" for structured protocol access (Feb 21, 2026).
- DeFAI systems have redeployed $2B+ in TVL across Aave, Morpho, Compound, Moonwell.
- Aave v4 and Lido v3 expected to launch in early 2026.
- 60% of crypto wallets projected to integrate agentic AI by end of 2026.
- Intent-based protocols allow users to declare desired outcomes; solver networks compete to execute.

**Why it matters:**
- DeFi is automating itself. Users increasingly delegate execution to AI agents. This creates demand for secure agent execution infrastructure, solver networks, and intent-routing layers.

**Sector:** DeFi, yield optimization, portfolio automation, UX abstraction

**Who buys/uses:** Retail DeFi users, protocols, DAOs, treasury managers

**Startup attractiveness:** INCREASES. Execution infrastructure, agent-wallet security, and solver marketplaces are early but high-conviction bets. Risk: fragmentation across intent standards.

### 4. ZK / COMPLIANCE TOOLING

**What happened:**
- ZK rollup TVL exceeded $28B.
- ZK-KYC emerging as primary compliance primitive: prove regulatory status without revealing personal data.
- Ethereum's 2025-2026 roadmap includes zk-STARKs for quantum resistance.
- Succinct's Open Prover Layer (OPL) creates a marketplace for outsourced proving.
- ZKsync Prividium evolving into bank-grade privacy infrastructure.
- Ethereum Foundation disbursed $7.38M in Q4 2025 to 136 projects, significant ZK focus.

**Why it matters:**
- MiCA and GENIUS Act both tighten compliance requirements. Institutions need privacy + auditability simultaneously. ZK is the only technical solution. Growing regulatory pressure = growing demand for ZK compliance tools.

**Sector:** Compliance, identity, privacy, scaling

**Who buys/uses:** Banks, DeFi protocols, regulated crypto businesses, governments

**Startup attractiveness:** INCREASES. zk-KYC, zk-compliance middleware, and prover marketplaces are high-moat plays. Technical barrier is a feature for founders with ZK expertise.

### 5. CROSS-CHAIN INFRASTRUCTURE

**What happened:**
- Chainlink CCIP gaining enterprise traction with 0.05% fee model and institutional reliability reputation.
- Stargate (LayerZero) supports 40 chains; Axelar supports 50+; Meson Finance supports 60 networks.
- Bridge hack losses cumulative exceeded $2.8B; security remains the sector's biggest risk.
- MiCA requires bridge operators to be licensed as service providers by July 2026.
- FinCEN expanding Travel Rule monitoring to cross-chain flows.

**Why it matters:**
- Bridges are necessary but dangerous infrastructure. The gap between protocol maturity and security remains wide. Regulatory compliance layer is now required in EU.

**Sector:** Interoperability, liquidity routing, cross-border settlement

**Who buys/uses:** DeFi protocols, exchanges, institutional traders, enterprises

**Startup attractiveness:** MODERATE. Core bridge protocols are commoditizing. Opportunity lies in security tooling on top of bridges, compliance wrappers, and intent-routing that abstracts bridge selection.
